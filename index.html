earlylife

Steve Jobs was born on February 24, 1955, in San Francisco, California, to Joanne Schieble and Abdulfattah Jandali, two University of Wisconsin graduate students who put him up for adoption. He was adopted by Paul and Clara Jobs, and grew up in Mountain View, California. As a child, Jobs was intelligent and curious, with a love for electronics and gadgets. He often experimented with electronics, taking them apart and putting them back together to see how they worked. He attended Homestead High School in Cupertino, where he met Steve Wozniak, who would later become his business partner. Jobs was not a particularly good student, but he was interested in spirituality and dropped out of college to explore his interests in Eastern mysticism and personal growth.

roadto

becomingaCEO

1972

Jobs enrolls at Reed College in Portland, Oregon but drops out after six months.

1974

Jobs returns to California and starts attending meetings of the Homebrew Computer Club, where he meets Steve Wozniak.

1975

Jobs and Wozniak develop the first commercially successful personal computer, the Apple I.

1976

Apple Computer is founded by Jobs, Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne. The company is based out of Jobs' parents' garage.

1977

The Apple II is released and becomes a huge success, making Jobs and Wozniak millionaires.

1980

Apple goes public, and Jobs becomes a multimillionaire at the age of 25.

1983

Jobs lures John Sculley from PepsiCo to be CEO of Apple, famously asking him, "Do you want to sell sugar water for the rest of your life, or do you want to come with me and change the world?"

1984

Apple launches the Macintosh computer with the famous "1984" ad directed by Ridley Scott

1985

After a power struggle with the Apple board of directors, Jobs resigns from the company and starts a new computer company called NeXT.

1986

Jobs buys Pixar Animation Studios from George Lucas for $10 million.

1996

Apple buys NeXT, and Jobs returns to Apple as an advisor.

1997

Jobs becomes interim CEO of Apple and begins turning the company around.

2000

Apple releases the iMac and starts a string of successful product launches.

2001

Apple releases the iPod, which revolutionizes the music industry.

2003

Apple opens the iTunes Store, which becomes the largest music retailer in the world.

2007

Apple releases the iPhone, which revolutionizes the smartphone industry.

2010

Apple releases the iPad, which creates the tablet market.

2011

Steve resigned as the CEO of Apple due to health issues amd died later that year.
